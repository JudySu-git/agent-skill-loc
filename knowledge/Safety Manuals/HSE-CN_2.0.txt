1. PURPOSE
================================================================================
This procedure is established to prevent safety accidents, eliminate potential
occupational injuries, ensure the safety and health of all employees and related
parties in KION China, comply with all applicable national and international
occupational health and safety laws and regulations, and satisfy the requirements
of the SCA-German Safety Compliance Audit Standards. It establishes comprehensive
controls for all significant safety hazards identified in the company's operations.

================================================================================
2. SCOPE
================================================================================
2.1  Applicable to all KION China Plants (Linde (China), Linde (China) Zhangzhou
     Branch, KION Baoli, KION (Jinan)) and all KION Regions.

2.2  Applies to all employees regardless of employment type (full-time, part-time,
     contract, temporary, agency workers, and apprentices).

2.3  Covers the following processes:
     - Occupational health operation management
     - Safe operation management
     - Environmental operation management
     - Incident investigation management
     - Physical agent exposure management
     - Chemical and hazardous substance management
     - Emergency preparedness and response

================================================================================
3. DEFINITIONS
================================================================================
3.1  HSE Department: The department or team responsible for occupational health,
     safety, and environment.

3.2  Occupational Disease: Disease resulting from exposure to dust, radioactive
     substances, or other toxic and harmful substances during work activities.

3.3  PPE (Personal Protective Equipment): Personal protective equipment provided
     by the company to avoid or reduce accident injury and occupational hazards.

3.4  Interested Party: Any person or organization that can affect, be affected by,
     or perceive itself to be affected by a decision or activity, including
     suppliers, contractors, customers, owners, regulators, and community.

3.5  Special Equipment: Equipment involving significant safety risk, including
     boilers, pressure vessels (including gas cylinders), pressure pipelines,
     elevators, cranes/lifting appliances, and special factory vehicles (forklifts,
     bridge cranes).

3.6  Dangerous Chemical: Chemical with toxicity, corrosivity, explosivity, or
     flammability that may harm health, facilities, or the environment.

3.7  SDS/MSDS: Safety Data Sheet / Material Safety Data Sheet — chemical safety
     documentation required under applicable chemical regulations.

3.8  Hazard Assessment: A systematic documented evaluation of workplace hazards
     covering design, equipment, materials, processes, and work methods, with
     documented control measures.

3.9  Permit-to-Work (PTW): A formal written authorisation that must be issued and
     signed before high-risk work begins, confirming all protective measures are
     in place.

3.10 Confined Space: An enclosed or partially enclosed space not designed for
     continuous occupancy, with restricted entry/exit, including tanks, silos,
     shafts, pits, trenches, ditches, ducts, and pipelines.

3.11 ATEX Zone: Area classified by the probability of explosive atmosphere
     formation (gas zones 0/1/2; dust zones 20/21/22) under the Explosive
     Atmospheres Directive.

3.12 LEL: Lower Explosion Limit — the minimum concentration of a flammable
     substance in air at which ignition can occur.

3.13 CMR Substances: Carcinogenic, Mutagenic, or Reprotoxic substances classified
     under applicable chemical regulations.

3.14 LOTO (Lockout/Tagout): Energy isolation procedure ensuring equipment is
     de-energised and cannot be re-energised before maintenance is completed.

3.15 Competent Person: A person with sufficient training, experience, and
     knowledge to identify hazards and make judgements about their management.

3.16 OHS Committee: Occupational Health and Safety Committee — the formal
     worker-management consultation body for safety matters.

================================================================================
4. RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================
4.1  HSE Department:
     - Arrange occupational health examinations and manage health records
     - Develop, maintain, and train all HSE emergency plans
     - Conduct and document hazard assessments and risk reviews
     - Manage the permit-to-work system
     - Monitor physical agent exposures (noise, vibration, radiation, heat)
     - Maintain the OHS Committee secretariat
     - Report performance metrics and drive continuous improvement
     - Maintain CMR exposure registers and chemical inventory

4.2  Each Department:
     - Fulfil all HSE legal obligations within their area of responsibility
     - Implement local hazard controls and update risk assessments
     - Ensure employees complete required training before starting work
     - Report all incidents, near-misses, and hazardous conditions immediately
     - Participate in OHS Committee meetings

4.3  Safety Team / Worker Representatives:
     - Participate in accident investigation, safety inspections, and safety
       scheme discussions
     - Collect and escalate safety opinions from employees
     - Represent employees in OHS Committee consultations

4.4  All Employees:
     - Comply with all safety rules, procedures, and instructions
     - Report hazards, near-misses, and incidents immediately
     - Attend all required training and health examinations
     - Use PPE correctly and maintain it in good condition
     - Not work while impaired by alcohol, drugs, or medication

4.5  Site/Facility Manager:
     - Ensure qualified supervision for all work activities
     - Confirm all protective measures are in place before high-risk work begins
     - Maintain safe access, egress, and workplace conditions

5. PROCESS
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1 GENERAL PRINCIPLES AND HAZARD ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.1.1  All departments shall identify, assess, and control hazards in their area
       using the documented hazard assessment procedure. Assessments must cover:
       - Workplace design, layout, and equipment
       - Materials and substances in use
       - Work processes, methods, and human factors
       - Normal operations, maintenance, and emergency situations

5.1.2  Hazard assessments shall:
       - Be documented with identified hazards, risk ratings, and control measures
       - Apply the hierarchy of controls: Elimination → Substitution →
         Engineering Controls → Administrative Controls → PPE (in that order)
       - Prioritise collective protection over individual protection
       - Be completed before work begins and before any significant change

5.1.3  Hazard assessments shall be reviewed and updated:
       - After any accident, near-miss, or occupational illness
       - After changes to equipment, processes, materials, or layout
       - After introduction of new work methods or products
       - At the request of the HSE department or regulatory authority
       - At least annually

5.1.4  Control measures from hazard assessments shall be documented, assigned to
       a responsible person, and tracked to completion.

5.1.5  The three-simultaneities principle (simultaneous design, construction, and
       acceptance) applies to all new facilities, major renovations, and projects.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.2 OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.2.1  Health Examinations

       a) Pre-employment: Health examinations required before assignment to any
          role with occupational hazard exposure.
       b) Periodic: Annual health examinations for all employees; bi-annual for
          employees working night shifts or with significant hazard exposure.
       c) Job transfer: Examination required before and after transfer to/from a
          hazardous role.
       d) Departure (including retirees): Exit health examination required for
          employees who have been exposed to occupational hazards.
       e) Hazard-specific examinations: Additional examinations required for
          exposure to noise (hearing), vibration, chemicals, radiation, heat,
          respiratory hazards, and CMR substances at intervals specified by the
          relevant sub-procedure.
       f) Medical examination results shall be communicated to each employee.
       g) Findings requiring action (job reassignment, treatment, reduced hours)
          shall be acted upon by management based on physician recommendation.
       h) Examination findings for occupational disease cases shall be reported
          to the competent regulatory/labor inspectorate authority.
       i) Refer to: HSE-09-O Occupational Disease Management Regulation.

5.2.2  Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

       a) A documented PPE hazard assessment shall identify: which hazards exist,
          what PPE type and specification is required, and which tasks require PPE.
          Assessments shall be updated when task conditions change.
       b) PPE shall be selected to match the identified hazard and workplace
          environmental conditions. Selection criteria shall be documented.
       c) PPE shall be provided by the company at no cost to the employee.
       d) PPE shall be properly fitted to each individual wearer.
       e) Employees shall be trained on correct PPE use before first use and at
          least annually thereafter. Training shall be documented.
       f) PPE shall be inspected regularly; defective PPE shall be removed from
          service and replaced immediately.
       g) Employees shall not be permitted to work without required PPE.
       h) Refer to: HSE-11-O Personal Protective Equipment Management Regulation.

5.2.3  First Aid

       a) First aid kits shall be provided on each office floor and in each
          production workshop, accessible at all times. Locations shall be clearly
          marked and communicated to all employees.
       b) Each department and area shall designate at least one trained first aider
          (minimum 16-hour certified first aid training) per workforce size and
          risk level as determined by the HSE department.
       c) First aider names, company emergency number, and local medical emergency
          number shall be posted in the rest area, on bulletin boards, or on the
          first aid kit.
       d) First aid supplies shall be maintained in stock and inspected regularly.
       e) Refer to: HSE-10-O Emergency First-Aid Management Regulation.

5.2.4  Respiratory Protection

       a) Respiratory protective equipment (RPE) shall be provided where hazardous
          substance concentrations or oxygen deficiency risks exist.
       b) RPE shall be selected to reduce inhalation exposure below occupational
          exposure limits.
       c) Employees shall be trained on RPE use, maintenance, proper donning and
          doffing before first use.
       d) Donning/doffing drills with functional checks shall be conducted at
          least every six (6) months for all users. Drills shall be documented.
       e) RPE shall be inspected at least quarterly and after each use.
          Defective devices shall be withdrawn and replaced.
       f) A medical fitness examination shall be completed before assignment to
          frequent or prolonged RPE use.

5.2.5  Occupational Health Records

       a) The HSE department shall maintain occupational health records for all
          employees exposed to occupational hazards.
       b) Records shall include: employee identity, role, hazard exposure type,
          dates of exposure, examination dates, findings, and any actions taken.
       c) Health records shall be retained for a minimum of five (5) years
          after cessation of exposure, or longer as required by applicable law.
       d) Employee health information shall be handled confidentially.

5.3 SAFE OPERATION MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.3.1  General Safety Responsibilities

       Each department shall:
       - Implement the safety policy "Safety First, Precaution Crucial,
         Treatment Comprehensive"
       - Maintain area-specific safety responsibility systems
       - Enforce all safety operating procedures
       - Ensure employees do not work while impaired by alcohol, drugs, or
         any substance that could compromise their own safety or others
       - Refer to: HSE-17-O Occupational Health & Safety Management Regulation.

5.3.2  Contractor and Visitor Safety

       a) No contractor, visitor, or other third party shall enter the production
          area without authorisation from the responsible department.
       b) The HSE department shall communicate HSE requirements to all contractors
          before work commences. Contractors must sign acknowledgement.
       c) Host departments shall share workplace hazard information with all
          contractors and their employees before work begins.
       d) Contractors must comply with all applicable requirements of this
          procedure. Contractor safety performance shall be monitored.
       e) Training and site safety induction shall be provided to all external
          workers before they begin work on company premises.
       f) Refer to: HSE-14-O Interested Party EHS Management Regulation;
          HSE-15-O Contractor EHS Management Regulation;
          HSE-16-O Workshop Visit Safety Management Regulation.

5.3.3  High-Risk Work Permit System

       The following high-risk operations require a written Permit-to-Work (PTW)
       approved by both the responsible department manager AND the HSE department
       before work commences. All stated protective measures must be confirmed
       in place before approval is granted:

       - Confined space entry
       - Hot work (welding, cutting, grinding, open flame)
       - Work at height (>2 m fall risk)
       - Electrical live work or work on de-energised systems
       - Explosive atmosphere areas (ATEX zones)
       - Excavations deeper than 1.25 m
       - Temporary electrical supply installation
       - Equipment installation or major maintenance
       - Any other work identified in the hazard assessment as requiring PTW

       PTW forms shall be retained for at least five (5) years.

5.3.4  Confined Space Entry

       a) All confined spaces (tanks, silos, shafts, pits, trenches, ducts,
          pipelines) shall be identified and documented before work begins.
       b) A written Confined Space Entry Permit shall be issued by the responsible
          supervisor before any entry. The permit shall specify: identified hazards,
          required protective measures, and the named responsible person.
       c) Atmospheric testing is mandatory before entry and continuously during
          work to verify: no oxygen deficiency (<19.5%), no flammable gas
          concentration above 10% LEL, and no toxic substance above exposure limits.
          Oxygen shall NOT be used for ventilation purposes.
       d) Fresh air ventilation shall be established and maintained throughout the
          operation. Ventilation effectiveness shall be verified.
       e) A trained attendant shall remain at the entry point throughout the
          operation and shall not enter the space.
       f) A reliable communication system (radio/telephone) shall be established
          between the entrant and attendant when direct voice contact is not possible.
       g) Rescue equipment (harness, lifeline, winch or hoist capable of extracting
          the entrant from above) shall be provided before entry.
       h) Entry shall only be authorised after the responsible person confirms all
          protective measures are in place.
       i) Notification shall be submitted to the relevant authority for operations
          requiring written protective measures.

5.3.5  Electrical Safety

       a) Electrical work shall only be performed by qualified electricians, or by
          instructed persons under direct supervision of a qualified electrician.
       b) Electrical equipment shall be suitable for its intended use and
          environmental conditions (mechanical, thermal, chemical influences).
       c) Live parts shall be insulated, enclosed, or otherwise guarded to prevent
          direct contact.
       d) All electrical installations shall include at least one effective fault
          protection measure against indirect contact.
       e) De-energised Work (LOTO): Before commencing any electrical maintenance
          or repair work:
          - The work area shall be clearly defined
          - Circuits shall be isolated, locked out, and tagged
          - Absence of voltage shall be verified
          - Adjacent live parts shall be covered or safeguarded
          - LOTO devices shall not be removed by anyone other than the person
            who applied them
       f) Inspection schedule:
          - Construction site electrical installations: visual inspection at least
            weekly for visible defects
          - All installations: tested before commissioning and after modification
            or repair
          - Testing shall verify: basic protection, fault protection, and
            effectiveness of additional protective measures
       g) Electrical installations shall be tested and certified before first use.
       h) Personnel performing live electrical work shall have specialised training
          and receive annual refresher training. Required safety distances and PPE
          for live work shall be specified before work begins.

5.3.6  Fall Protection and Work at Height

       a) Fall protection measures shall be provided wherever:
          - Fall height exceeds 1 m at walls, stairs, and machine platforms
          - Fall height exceeds 2 m at general workplaces and traffic routes
       b) Hierarchy of fall protection (must be applied in this order):
          1. Guardrails, barriers, or covers (collective protection — preferred)
          2. Safety nets or catchment platforms
          3. Personal fall arrest systems (harness/lanyard) — only where
             collective measures are not feasible
       c) Openings in floors, roofs, or elevated surfaces shall be guarded by
          load-bearing covers, guardrails, or equivalent protective measures.
          Where covers are not possible, clear hazard marking is required.
       d) Where persons may be endangered by falling objects, protective roofs,
          barriers, or restricted zones shall be established below work areas.
       e) When working near unprotected edges, appropriate safety distances,
          barriers, or clearly marked restricted zones shall be maintained.
       f) Access to edge protection barrier zones shall be limited to operational
          necessity only.
       g) Employees working at height shall be trained in fall protection measures
          before beginning work.

5.3.7  Ladder Safety

       a) Ladders shall only be used for access/egress or short-duration light
          work where other means are impractical.
       b) Ladders shall be positioned at the correct angle (approximately 75°
          or 1 horizontal : 4 vertical).
       c) Ladder feet shall be secured against slipping. The top shall be
          secured or held by another person when in use.
       d) Both hands shall be free for climbing. Tools and materials shall be
          carried in a belt or hoisted separately.
       e) Roof ladders shall only be used where specifically designed for that
          purpose; standard ladders shall not be used on roofs.
       f) Ladders shall be inspected before each use and removed from service
          if damaged.

5.3.8  Roof Work

       a) Fall protection measures shall be provided for all roof work:
          - Roofs pitched ≤20°: guardrails or fall-arrest systems at all open
            edges where fall height exceeds 3 m
          - Roofs pitched >20° to ≤45°: secured walkways or equivalent required
          - Roofs pitched >45°: special access methods (fixed rungs, crawl boards,
            or equivalent) required
       b) A minimum of two (2) workers shall be present for high-risk roof work
          with fall heights exceeding 5 m.
       c) Work on roofs for more than five (5) working days with fall height >5 m
          shall be notified to the relevant authority before commencement.
       d) Fragile roof surfaces shall be clearly marked and fitted with
          load-bearing covers or walkways before access is permitted.
       e) Qualified personnel shall supervise all roof work activities.

5.3.9  Scaffolding

       a) Scaffolding shall be erected, altered, and dismantled only by qualified
          and competent personnel.
       b) Scaffolding shall be designed and erected to meet required load limits.
          Maximum load capacity shall be clearly marked.
       c) Scaffolding shall be inspected before each use (shift) by a competent
          person. Inspection records shall be maintained.
       d) Access to scaffolding shall be restricted until a competent person
          confirms it is safe for use.
       e) Employees using scaffolding shall be trained in its safe use before
          commencing work.

5.3.10 Excavation Safety

       a) Before any excavation begins, all underground utilities (pipes,
          cables, water, gas) and soil hazard conditions shall be identified.
       b) For excavations deeper than 1.25 m, an approved protective method
          shall be selected and implemented: sloping, shoring, or ground
          stabilisation.
       c) A 50 cm edge loading restriction shall be maintained around excavation
          edges unless specific collapse-prevention measures are in place.
       d) Loose materials, rocks, or structural elements at excavation faces
          shall be removed or secured to prevent falling.
       e) Workers shall not enter an excavation until sloping, shoring, or
          stabilisation measures are fully in place and confirmed by a
          competent person.
       f) Inspections shall be conducted: before work begins each day, after
          any event affecting stability (heavy rain, blasting, vibration), and
          after any work interruption exceeding one shift.
       g) All excavation work shall be supervised by a competent person.

5.3.11 Fire Safety

       a) A Fire Safety Officer shall be appointed where required by law or by
          risk assessment. Responsibilities include: employee fire behaviour
          briefing, fire self-monitoring, incipient fire response, evacuation
          co-ordination, and preparation for fire brigade operations.
       b) Designated fire response persons familiar with fire extinguisher use
          and evacuation procedures shall be appointed in each area.
       c) Fire prevention measures shall be established to prevent, control, and
          respond to fire hazards, including: ignition source controls, vapor
          accumulation prevention, and per-shift flammable waste removal.
       d) Suitable fire extinguishing equipment (appropriate to fire class and
          facility size) shall be provided, clearly marked, and kept accessible.
       e) Fire extinguishers and fixed suppression systems shall be inspected
          at least every two (2) years by a competent person. Inspection records
          shall be retained for at least three (3) years.
       f) Annual fire and evacuation drills shall be conducted for all occupied
          facilities. Drills shall be documented including attendance, findings,
          and remedial actions.
       g) A fire safety logbook shall record: inspections, deficiencies, drills,
          and any fires. Records shall be retained for at least three (3) years.
       h) Each factory shall comply with all applicable fire protection laws,
          regulations, and national standards.

5.3.12 Hot Work

       a) All hot work (welding, cutting, grinding, brazing, open-flame
          operations) requires a Hot Work Permit approved by the department
          manager and HSE before work begins.
       b) Gas cylinders shall be secured upright at all times and stored
          separately from oxygen and fuel gases when not in use. Valves shall
          be closed when not in use.
       c) The work area shall be cleared of flammable materials to a radius of
          at least 10 m before hot work begins. Flammable surfaces shall be
          wetted or protected with non-combustible blankets.
       d) A fire extinguisher shall be immediately available at the hot work site.
       e) After hot work is completed, a fire watch shall be maintained for a
          minimum of 30 minutes (or longer as determined by risk assessment).
       f) Hot work in explosive atmosphere zones is prohibited unless the area
          has been fully classified as safe and the ATEX permit is also in place.
       g) Annual training on hot work safety, gas cylinder handling, and fire
          prevention shall be provided to all employees who perform hot work.

5.3.13 Explosive Atmospheres (ATEX)

       a) An explosion hazard assessment shall be conducted for all areas where
          flammable gases, vapours, mists, or dusts may be present. The assessment
          shall address: probability and duration of explosive atmosphere formation,
          properties of flammable substances, and potential ignition sources.
       b) An Explosion Protection Document shall be created before work begins in
          any area with explosion risk. The document shall be kept up to date.
       c) Explosive atmosphere areas shall be classified into zones:
          - Zone 0 (gas): explosive atmosphere present continuously
          - Zone 1 (gas): explosive atmosphere present occasionally during
            normal operation
          - Zone 2 (gas): explosive atmosphere not likely in normal operation
          - Zone 20/21/22: equivalent zones for combustible dust
       d) Only equipment rated and certified for the applicable ATEX zone shall
          be used in classified areas. Zone markings shall be posted.
       e) A competent person shall issue a Work Release before any work in
          classified zones, confirming all controls are in place.
       f) Atmospheric monitoring shall measure flammable substance concentration
          to verify primary explosion protection effectiveness. Where concentrations
          exceed 25% LEL, annual control measurements are mandatory.
       g) Electrical installations in explosive atmosphere zones shall be
          inspected at least every three (3) years.
       h) Annual training on explosion hazards, zone classification, safe equipment
          use, work practices, and required PPE shall be provided to all workers
          in or near classified zones.

5.3.14 Special Equipment and Cranes

       a) Special equipment operators (cranes, forklifts, pressure vessels,
          elevators) must hold valid operating certificates. Certificates shall
          be verified before assignment.
       b) Special equipment safety management systems shall be implemented per
          HSE-28-O Special Equipment Use Safety Management Regulation.
       c) Crane-specific requirements:
          - Pre-use daily inspection by a qualified person before each shift
          - Load capacity limits shall be posted on each crane; limits shall
            not be exceeded
          - Restricted access zones shall be established under suspended loads
          - A banksman/signal person shall direct crane movements when the
            operator's view is obstructed
          - Communication (radio or hand signals) shall be established before
            each lift
          - Cranes shall be tested before commissioning and after any
            structural modification or repair

5.3.15 Lifting Operations and Hoists

       a) Pre-lift inspection shall be completed before each lifting operation,
          verifying: load attachment, sling condition, hook latch, and rated
          capacity.
       b) Load attachment points shall be confirmed secured before any lift.
          Loads shall not be left suspended unattended.
       c) Hoists shall be securely installed, marked with maximum load capacity,
          and have guarded moving parts.
       d) Restricted access shall be maintained below hoist travel paths during
          operation.

5.3.16 Manual Handling and Material Storage

       a) The need for manual handling shall be minimised through mechanisation,
          trolleys, lift assists, or work area redesign.
       b) A manual handling risk assessment shall be conducted for tasks involving
          repeated lifting, carrying, pushing, or pulling.
       c) Maximum manual handling load limits shall be defined in the assessment.
          Loads exceeding limits require mechanical assistance.
       d) Workers shall be trained in safe manual handling techniques before
          performing such tasks.
       e) Material storage requirements:
          - Stacked materials shall not exceed 2 m in height unless specific
            engineering controls are in place
          - Stacks shall be founded on stable, level surfaces
          - Heavy items shall be stored at lower levels
          - Storage areas shall be inspected regularly and kept clear of hazards
          - Authorised load zones shall be defined and marked

5.3.17 Traffic Safety

       a) Designated traffic routes shall be established and clearly marked for
          vehicle and pedestrian movement within all factory and site areas.
       b) Traffic routes shall provide minimum clearances of:
          - Width: sufficient for vehicle type plus 0.5 m each side
          - Height: at least 2.0 m above the highest point of vehicles using
            the route
       c) Vehicle and pedestrian routes shall be physically separated wherever
          practicable.
       d) Workers in areas with vehicle traffic shall wear high-visibility (hi-vis)
          clothing at all times.
       e) Traffic routes shall be maintained in good condition, free from
          obstructions and surface hazards.
       f) Traffic management measures shall be in place to control vehicle
          movement near worksites and conveyors.

5.3.18 Working On, Over, or Near Water

       a) Employees working on, over, or near water shall wear appropriate PPE
          including personal flotation devices.
       b) At least one trained rescue person shall be present and on standby
          whenever work over water is in progress.
       c) A manned motorboat or rescue craft shall be available where required
          by risk assessment.
       d) Annual water rescue drills shall be conducted and documented for all
          teams with regular water work exposure.
       e) All employees assigned to water work shall be trained in water safety
          and rescue procedures before commencing such work.

5.3.19 Demolition

       a) A structural evaluation by a competent engineer shall be conducted
          before any demolition work commences to assess stability risks.
       b) All hazardous materials (asbestos, lead paint, PCBs) shall be
          identified and safely removed by certified contractors before any
          structural demolition begins.
       c) All utilities (electricity, gas, water, telecommunications) shall be
          isolated, disconnected, and capped before demolition.
       d) Debris control measures (screens, containment, restricted access
          zones) shall be in place throughout demolition.
       e) Access to demolition areas shall be restricted to authorised workers
          with appropriate PPE.
       f) Demolition work shall be supervised by a competent person at all times.

5.3.20 Abrasive Blasting

       a) Abrasive materials containing more than 2% free crystalline silica
          (quartz) are prohibited for blasting operations.
       b) Dust control measures (wet blasting, local exhaust ventilation, or
          equivalent) shall be implemented during all blasting operations.
       c) Respiratory protection (minimum P3/FFP3 or supplied-air respirator
          as determined by exposure assessment) shall be provided and worn
          during blasting.
       d) Access to blast areas shall be restricted and posted with hazard
          warning signs during operations.
       e) Notification shall be submitted to the relevant authority before
          blasting operations exceeding the statutory threshold.

5.3.21 Road Transport of Dangerous Goods

       a) All dangerous goods transported by road shall be classified and
          authorised in accordance with applicable transport regulations (ADR
          or equivalent national standard).
       b) Appropriate transport documents shall accompany all dangerous goods
          shipments.
       c) Approved packaging, containers, and tanks shall be used for all
          dangerous goods. Packages shall be correctly marked and placarded.
       d) Vehicles transporting dangerous goods shall be equipped with mandatory
          emergency equipment (fire extinguisher, first aid kit, eye wash,
          emergency information card).
       e) Drivers transporting dangerous goods shall hold valid training
          certificates for the categories of goods carried.
       f) Dangerous goods transport vehicles shall be inspected before each
          journey.

5.4 PHYSICAL AGENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.4.1  Noise

       a) Noise hazard identification shall be conducted for all areas where
          significant noise exposure cannot be ruled out. Workplace noise
          measurements shall be conducted considering both continuous and
          impulse noise. Measurements shall be repeated after any significant
          change to equipment or work processes.
       b) Exposure limits:
          - Action level: 80 dBA (8-hour average) — controls required
          - Limit value: 85 dBA (8-hour average) — must not be exceeded
          - Peak sound pressure: 135 dBC must not be exceeded
       c) When the 80 dBA action level is exceeded:
          - Noise reduction measures shall be implemented (engineering then
            administrative then hearing protection)
          - Employees shall be enrolled in hearing surveillance
          - Hearing protection shall be made available
          - Noise areas shall be marked and, where justified, demarcated
       d) When the 85 dBA limit is exceeded:
          - Immediate corrective action is required
          - Use of hearing protection is mandatory
          - Restricted access control shall be enforced
       e) Pre-assignment hearing examinations shall be conducted before
          assigning workers to tasks with hazardous noise exposure.
          Periodic hearing examinations shall follow at frequency determined
          by risk level.
       f) A noise exposure register shall be maintained listing all employees
          exposed above action levels. The register shall be kept current and
          submitted to insurance authorities when required.
       g) Noise training shall be provided: at initial assignment and whenever
          action level exceedance is identified. Training shall cover: health
          risks, limits, measurement results, controls, and PPE.
       h) Refer to: HSE-13-O Pollutant and Noise Emission Management Regulation.

5.4.2  Vibration

       a) Vibration exposure limits (must not be exceeded, 8-hour average):
          - Hand-arm vibration (HAV): 5 m/s²
          - Whole-body vibration (WBV): 1.15 m/s²
       b) Vibration action values (controls required when exceeded):
          - Hand-arm: 2.5 m/s²
          - Whole-body: 0.5 m/s²
       c) Representative vibration measurements shall be conducted wherever
          assessments cannot reliably exclude exceedance of action values.
       d) When action values are exceeded:
          - Engineering controls shall be applied to reduce vibration at source
            (anti-vibration mountings, suspension seats, low-vibration tools)
          - Work rotation or rest breaks shall be introduced
          - Health surveillance shall be initiated
       e) When limit values are exceeded, immediate corrective action is required.
       f) High-vibration areas shall be marked; restricted access applied where
          justified.
       g) Training on vibration risks, controls, limits, health effects, and safe
          work methods shall be provided when action values are exceeded.

5.4.3  Heat Stress and UV Radiation

       a) A heat stress risk assessment shall be conducted for all outdoor work
          and indoor work in hot environments.
       b) A documented heat and UV action programme shall be established based
          on the risk assessment, covering technical, organisational, and personal
          protective measures. The programme shall be activated when weather
          authorities issue a heat warning at or above the threshold equivalent
          to significant heat stress risk (≥Level 2 or equivalent national alert).
       c) Technical and organisational heat control measures shall include:
          - Scheduling heavy work during cooler hours
          - Providing shaded rest areas with adequate ventilation
          - Providing cool drinking water and rest breaks
          - Acclimatisation programme for workers new to hot work
          - Reduced work/rest ratios during high heat alerts
       d) First aid measures for heat-related illness (heat exhaustion, heat
          stroke) shall be defined and communicated to all workers. At least
          one first aider trained in heat illness response shall be present.
       e) UV radiation controls for outdoor workers shall include: UV-protective
          clothing, hats, sunscreen (SPF 30+), and scheduling to avoid peak
          UV hours (typically 10:00–15:00).
       f) Training on heat and UV hazards, warning signs of heat illness, and
          protective measures shall be provided before and during the hot season.
       g) Medical examinations addressing heat strain shall be provided for
          workers with significant heat exposure.

5.4.4  Ionising Radiation

       a) Ionising radiation exposure shall be assessed for all work involving
          radioactive sources, X-ray equipment, or naturally occurring
          radioactive materials (NORM).
       b) Radiation dose limits shall comply with applicable national regulations.
          Workers shall not exceed annual effective dose limits.
       c) Engineering controls (shielding, distance, enclosure) shall be
          applied to reduce exposure to as low as reasonably achievable (ALARA).
       d) Access to radiation areas shall be controlled. Areas shall be marked
          with ionising radiation warning symbols.
       e) Pre-work and periodic medical examinations (including bioassay where
          relevant) shall be conducted for all classified radiation workers.
       f) Annual training on radiation hazards, dose limits, controls, and
          emergency procedures shall be provided before first assignment and
          annually thereafter.
       g) Radiation workers shall be enrolled in a dose monitoring register.
          Personal dosimeters shall be worn and dose records retained.

5.4.5  Optical Radiation (Non-Ionising)

       a) A hazard assessment shall be conducted for all work involving
          significant optical radiation sources (lasers, arc welding, UV lamps,
          intense light sources).
       b) Exposure shall not exceed applicable limit values for the eyes and skin.
       c) Engineering controls (enclosures, interlocks, screens) shall be
          applied at source. Administrative controls (restricted access, warning
          signs) shall supplement engineering measures.
       d) Appropriate eye and face protection (laser safety goggles, welding
          helmets, UV-blocking eyewear) shall be provided and worn.
       e) Training on optical radiation hazards, exposure limits, controls, and
          PPE shall be provided to all affected workers.

5.4.6  Electromagnetic Fields (EMF)

       a) EMF exposure assessment shall be conducted for all work near high-power
          electrical equipment, large motors, induction heaters, MRI equipment,
          or welding machines.
       b) Exposure shall not exceed applicable action values or limit values.
       c) Where action values are exceeded, technical controls (shielding,
          increased distance, process optimisation) shall be implemented.
          Administrative controls (restricted access, rotation) shall supplement
          technical measures.
       d) PPE shall be provided where residual exposure risk remains after
          engineering and administrative controls.
       e) Training on EMF hazards, controls, and any relevant health effects
          shall be provided to all workers with significant EMF exposure.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.5 CHEMICAL AND HAZARDOUS SUBSTANCE MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.5.1  General Chemical Controls

       a) Safety Data Sheets (SDS/MSDS) shall be obtained for all hazardous
          chemicals before use. SDS shall be accessible to employees and
          regulatory authorities at all times. SDS shall be reviewed and updated
          when new versions are issued by the supplier.
       b) All substances used in the workplace shall be assessed for hazardous
          properties using CLP/GHS classification criteria. Substances meeting
          criteria for explosive, flammable, toxic, CMR, or biological hazard
          shall be managed under this procedure.
       c) A chemical inventory shall be maintained listing all hazardous substances
          on site, their locations, quantities, and SDS reference.
       d) Exposure assessment:
          - Worker exposure type, extent, and duration shall be assessed initially,
            periodically, after any process change, and when health concerns arise.
          - Exposure shall be maintained below applicable occupational exposure
            limits (OELs/MAK values). Where carcinogenic substances are involved,
            exposure shall be reduced to as low as reasonably practicable.
          - Measurement methods shall comply with applicable standards.
       e) Hierarchy of chemical controls:
          1. Elimination of the hazardous substance
          2. Substitution with a less hazardous alternative
          3. Engineering controls (local exhaust ventilation, enclosure)
          4. Administrative controls (reduced exposure time, rotation)
          5. PPE as last resort
       f) Containers and pipelines containing hazardous substances shall be
          permanently and clearly labelled with hazard markings (GHS/CLP symbols,
          signal words, hazard statements).
       g) Storage of hazardous substances:
          - Storage areas shall be appropriate for the hazard (ventilated,
            temperature-controlled, compatible materials separated)
          - Storage rooms and cabinets shall be marked with hazard symbols
          - Incompatible substances (e.g., oxidisers and flammables) shall be
            separated
       h) Residual chemicals shall not be discarded arbitrarily. They shall be
          returned to the warehouse or stored in a designated area for HSE
          disposal per HSE-13-O.
       i) Refer to: HSE-12-O Chemical Storage and Using Management Regulation.

5.5.2  Carcinogenic, Mutagenic, and Reprotoxic (CMR) Substances

       a) Before any CMR substance is first used in the workplace, a notification
          shall be submitted to the relevant regulatory/labor authority.
       b) Access to areas where CMR substances are handled shall be restricted
          to authorised and trained employees only.
       c) A CMR Exposure Register shall be maintained, listing:
          - Names of all workers potentially exposed to CMR substances
          - Names and CAS numbers of CMR substances
          - Nature of work and duration/frequency of exposure
          - Results of exposure measurements
       d) The exposure register shall be retained for at least 40 years (or as
          required by applicable law) given the long latency of associated diseases.
       e) Substitution of CMR substances with less hazardous alternatives shall
          be actively pursued. Use of CMR substances shall be minimised.
       f) Pre-employment and periodic health examinations shall be conducted for
          all workers exposed to CMR substances.

5.5.3  Lead and Lead Paint

       a) Materials containing more than 2% lead in surface coatings are prohibited
          for use in construction and maintenance activities.
       b) Where lead paint is present in existing structures (e.g., during
          renovation or demolition), it shall be identified and managed by
          qualified contractors using appropriate dust controls, respiratory
          protection, and hygiene provisions.
       c) Workers handling lead shall be provided with appropriate RPE and
          protective clothing. Eating, drinking, and smoking in lead work areas
          is strictly prohibited.
       d) Notification shall be submitted to the relevant authority before lead
          work expected to last more than five (5) working days.

5.5.4  Explosives

       a) Possession and use of explosives requires a valid permit from the
          relevant regulatory authority. No explosives shall be stored or used
          on company premises without current authorisation.
       b) Only qualified, licensed blasters shall handle or use explosives.
       c) Explosives shall be stored in approved, secure storage facilities
          complying with applicable regulations.
       d) An Explosives Officer shall be appointed for any project using explosives.
       e) Only approved disposal methods shall be used for expired or surplus
          explosives.
       f) Explosives records (purchase, use, stock, disposal) shall be retained
          for at least ten (10) years.

5.6 ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.6.1  The company shall comply with all applicable national and regional
       environmental laws and regulations regarding waste, emissions, and
       pollution control.

5.6.2  Effective control shall be implemented for exhaust gas, solid waste, noise,
       and wastewater emissions from all facilities.

5.6.3  The three-simultaneities principle applies to all new facilities and
       major process changes.

5.6.4  Hazardous waste shall be managed, stored, and disposed of through approved
       channels. Records of hazardous waste disposal shall be maintained.

5.6.5  Refer to: HSE-13-O Pollutant and Noise Emission Management Regulation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.7 EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS AND RESPONSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.7.1  The company shall maintain an emergency preparedness and response
       management system covering all foreseeable emergencies including: fire,
       chemical spill, explosion, medical emergency, power failure, flood,
       and natural disaster.

5.7.2  Emergency notification:
       - Precautions ensuring immediate employee notification in case of danger
         shall be in place at all times
       - Emergency alarm systems shall be tested regularly
       - Emergency contact numbers (HSE, fire brigade, medical services, local
         authority) shall be posted in all occupied areas

5.7.3  Escape routes:
       - At least one unobstructed escape route shall be accessible within 10 m
         of any workplace location
       - Escape routes shall be clearly marked with directional signage
       - Escape routes shall remain unobstructed at all times when employees
         are present
       - Emergency lighting shall be provided along all escape routes and in
         areas with no natural light, sufficient to support safe evacuation in
         darkness

5.7.4  Annual emergency drills:
       - At least one full emergency drill (evacuation + fire response) shall
         be conducted annually at each facility
       - Desktop/tabletop exercises shall supplement physical drills
       - All employees must participate
       - Drills shall be documented: date, participants, findings, remedial
         actions, and completion sign-off

5.7.5  Emergency equipment:
       - Fire extinguishers, first aid kits, eye wash stations, spill kits,
         and emergency lighting shall be maintained in serviceable condition
       - All emergency equipment shall be inspected regularly and defects
         corrected promptly

5.7.6  Chemical spill response:
       - Chemical-specific emergency plans shall be maintained
       - Spill kits appropriate to chemical hazards on site shall be readily
         available
       - All relevant employees shall be trained in spill response procedures

5.7.7  Refer to: HSE-18-O Emergency Preparation and Response Management Regulation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.8 INCIDENT RECORDING AND REPORTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.8.1  All employees are required to report immediately to their department head
       and the HSE department any:
       - Injury accident (however minor)
       - Near-miss event
       - Dangerous condition or safety defect
       - Occupational illness symptom

5.8.2  The HSE department shall investigate all reported incidents to identify
       root causes and implement corrective actions to prevent recurrence.

5.8.3  Accidents resulting in more than three (3) days of incapacity, any
       work-related fatality, and any diagnosed occupational disease shall be
       reported to the relevant accident insurance institution and regulatory
       authority within five (5) days of occurrence.

5.8.4  All accident records, investigation reports, and corrective action plans
       shall be retained for a minimum of five (5) years.

5.8.5  Permit-to-Work records shall be retained for a minimum of five (5) years.

5.8.6  Refer to: HSE-23-O Incidents Investigation Management Regulation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.9 WORKPLACE CONDITIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.9.1  Alcohol and Drug Prohibition:
       Employees shall not place themselves in a condition — due to alcohol,
       medication, or drugs — that compromises their own safety or the safety
       of others. Any employee found impaired shall be removed from work
       immediately. This applies to all employees including contractors.

5.9.2  Hazard Signage:
       - All workplace hazards (obstacles, holes, depressions, restricted zones,
         hazardous substance areas, noise zones, radiation areas, ATEX zones)
         shall be marked with appropriate warning signs and/or physical barriers
       - Signage shall comply with applicable national standards for colour,
         symbol, and format

5.9.3  Workplace Lighting:
       - All occupied areas shall be provided with adequate artificial lighting
         where natural light is insufficient
       - Minimum illumination: 30 lux in indoor areas; higher levels required
         for detailed or precision work
       - Glare shall be prevented through appropriate fixture selection and
         positioning
       - Emergency lighting shall be maintained as specified in Section 5.7.3

5.9.4  Ventilation:
       - All workrooms shall be provided with adequate ventilation appropriate
         to the intended use, occupancy, and any processes generating
         contaminants
       - Ventilation systems shall be inspected and maintained regularly

5.9.5  Hygiene Facilities:
       - Sufficient toilet facilities with flushing, toilet paper, and
         adjacent handwashing facilities shall be provided
       - Separate changing facilities and, where required, shower facilities
         shall be provided for workers in dirty or hazardous roles
       - Rest areas with seating shall be provided

5.9.6  Housekeeping:
       - All work areas shall be maintained in a clean, orderly condition
       - Walkways shall be kept clear at all times
       - Waste and debris shall be removed regularly

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.10 TRAINING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.10.1 Safety training shall be provided before work begins and repeated as
       needed. Specifically:
       a) Pre-employment induction: mandatory for all employees before starting
          work, covering site rules, emergency procedures, hazards, and PPE
       b) Job-change training: required before assignment to any new task with
          different hazard profile
       c) Hazard-specific training: required for all employees working with or
          near specific hazards (chemicals, noise, vibration, radiation, height,
          confined spaces, electrical, etc.) — frequency as specified in
          relevant sections of this procedure
       d) Supervisor/manager safety training: required for all supervisory staff
       e) Refresher training: required after incidents, after process changes,
          after new equipment introduction, and at intervals specified by
          applicable regulations

5.10.2 All training shall be:
       - Delivered during working hours
       - Conducted in a language and format that participants understand,
         with confirmed comprehension (e.g., written test or verbal check)
       - Documented with: date, trainer, attendees, topics covered, and
         sign-off by each participant
       - Records retained for at least five (5) years

5.10.3 Contractors and external workers shall receive site safety induction
       and hazard information relevant to their work before commencing
       operations on company premises.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.11 MULTI-EMPLOYER COORDINATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.11.1 Where multiple employers' workers share the same workplace, the host
       employer shall:
       - Co-ordinate hazard prevention activities among all employers
       - Share comprehensive workplace hazard information with all employer
         representatives and their workers before work begins
       - Agree on protective measures and ensure they do not create risks
         for the workers of other employers
       - Designate a co-ordination point of contact for safety matters

5.11.2 External employers shall:
       - Ensure their workers comply with the host employer's safety rules
       - Inform the host employer of any hazards their work creates
       - Provide hazard-specific training to their own workers

5.11.3 Refer to: HSE-14-O Interested Party EHS Management Regulation;
       HSE-15-O Contractor EHS Management Regulation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.12 OHS COMMITTEE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.12.1 An Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) Committee shall be established
       at all facilities with 100 or more workers. Smaller facilities should
       establish equivalent worker consultation mechanisms.

5.12.2 Committee composition:
       - HSE Director or designee (Committee Chair)
       - Department safety representatives (management side)
       - Elected worker representatives (at least equal in number to management
         representatives)
       - Industrial physician / occupational health adviser (advisory role)
       - HSE Secretariat (HR or HSE department)

5.12.3 The OHS Committee shall meet at least monthly. Extraordinary sessions
       shall be convened for urgent safety matters.

5.12.4 Mandatory agenda items:
       - Review of accidents, near-misses, and occupational diseases since
         last meeting
       - Review of outstanding corrective actions
       - Review of hazard assessment updates
       - Employee safety concerns and suggestions
       - Review of audit and inspection findings
       - Planning of drills, training, and health examinations

5.12.5 Meeting minutes shall be prepared and distributed to all members and
       posted for general employee information. Minutes shall be retained for
       at least three (3) years.

5.12.6 An Accident Response Committee shall be convened for any serious accident
       or dangerous occurrence to investigate root causes and develop
       recurrence prevention measures.

5.12.7 The OHS Committee secretariat shall be managed by the HSE department.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.13 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT AND CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5.13.1 HSE performance indicators shall be established, including leading and
       lagging metrics:
       - Leading: training completion rates, hazard assessment completion,
         inspection completion, near-miss reports
       - Lagging: accident frequency rate (AFR), severity rate, occupational
         disease incidence, audit findings

5.13.2 HSE performance shall be reviewed monthly by the OHS Committee and
       quarterly by senior management.

5.13.3 Corrective and preventive measures shall be implemented promptly when
       performance targets are not met or non-conformances are identified.
       Actions shall be tracked to completion.

5.13.4 The HSE management system shall undergo an internal audit at least
       annually. Results shall be reported to senior management.

5.13.5 Refer to: HSE-27-O HSE and Energy Performance Monitoring and Measuring
       Management Regulation.
